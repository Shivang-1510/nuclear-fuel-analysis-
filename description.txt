This module performs nuclear fuel energy analysis by calculating and visualizing the energy released per kilogram of various nuclear fuels during fission reactions.

Key Components:

Constants: Defines energy data for three fuels (U-235, Pu-239, Th-232), conversion factors (MeV to Joules), and atomic masses
Core Functions:
energy_per_kg(): Calculates total energy released per kilogram of fuel using atomic physics (atoms per kg × energy per fission)
calculate_all_fuels(): Processes all available fuels and returns their energy outputs
print_results(): Displays results in a formatted table
visualize_results(): Creates a bar chart comparing fuel energy outputs with optional saving
Main Execution: Runs the complete analysis pipeline—calculating, printing, and visualizing results
The analysis converts MeV energy values to Terajoules per kilogram, considering Avogadro's number and atomic masses for accurate calculations.
Nuclear fuel analysis is important for a country to setup its nuclear reactors as it is based on fuel availability and energy released by the fuels available.
